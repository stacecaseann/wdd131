Spanish Fork, UT

Spanish Fork was founded in 1855. It was attractive to the Native Americans, particularly the Ute people, because of the beautiful Spanish Fork River that flowed through the valley. In the late 1700's the Dominguez-Escalante expedition came through the area looking for a route from New Mexico to California. In 1851 a group of Mormons settled in the valley and it thrived and grew. Many Icelandic immigrants also settled here in the late 1800's and there is a monument built for them near the center of the city. Today Spanish Fork continues to grow and thrive.
